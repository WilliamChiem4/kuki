# KUKI — Product Strategy

*One perfect cookie, delivered warm. The app that turns that into a habit.*

---

## Thesis

Most bakery apps die the same way. They are checkout funnels wearing a logo — a menu, a cart, a card form. All of their value is concentrated in the ninety seconds around a purchase, so the moment the craving passes, there is no reason to open them. They get buried on the last home screen and deleted at the next storage warning. Ordering is a *transaction*, and transactions don't build habits.

KUKI's constraint is its advantage. We make **one** cookie. We can't win by being a bigger menu, so we don't try. Instead of an app you open to *buy*, we build an app you open to *belong* — membership in a baking studio. You open it to see what we're baking next, to claim a drop before it sells out, to watch your batch come out of the oven, to spend what you've earned. Buying is one thing that happens inside that relationship, not the whole relationship.

The retention engine is **scarcity + ritual + collection**:

- **Scarcity** — limited drops on a cadence create a reason to return on a schedule, not just on a craving.
- **Ritual** — live bake tracking turns every order into a small event, from "in the oven" to "at your door, still warm."
- **Collection** — a record of every flavor you've tried compounds over time and becomes something you'd show a friend.

The right mental models are **a sneaker-drop app and a record club** — anticipation, membership, and a growing collection — *not* Uber Eats. The signature cookie is always there, the dependable core. Everything else exists to make the app worth keeping between orders.

## 1. Core purpose

**Why someone downloads it.** App-exclusive access. The experimental flavor drops are app-first and time-boxed — you can only get them here, and only while they last. A signup cookie (a free or discounted first cookie) removes the friction of the very first order. The pitch is never "another way to order"; it's "the only way in."

**Why they keep it installed.** Three jobs the website can't do as well:

1. **Find out what KUKI is baking next.** The app is the home of the drop calendar and the next-flavor reveal. Anticipation is a feature.
2. **Watch the bake, live.** A premium take on the Domino's tracker: *"Your batch went in the oven at 2:14pm,"* then on its way, then warm at your door. The wait becomes part of the product.
3. **Hold a balance.** Your loyalty (Dough), your tier, your past flavors — a reason to come back that accrues whether or not you're hungry right now.

**Value beyond ordering.** Three things a checkout funnel never delivers:

- **Provenance** — the craft made legible: the brown butter, the hand-chopped 55% Belgian chocolate, the test-batch obsession. Why this cookie is *the* cookie.
- **Anticipation** — knowing a drop is coming, counting down to it, planning around it.
- **Collection** — a personal, growing record of what you've tasted that means more the longer you stay.

## 2. Personas

Four personas, arranged as a ladder. Each has a job to be done, one primary retention hook, and a clear graduation path to the next.

### Casual customer
- **Who:** ordered once or twice, probably from the website or a friend's recommendation. Likes the cookie; not yet attached to the brand.
- **Job to be done:** "I want a genuinely great cookie without thinking too hard about it."
- **Primary retention hook:** the live bake tracker — a delightful, memorable first ordering experience that's noticeably better than any delivery app.
- **Graduates when:** they catch a drop announcement and realize there's something here you can't get any other day. Anticipation pulls them up the ladder.

### Cookie enthusiast
- **Who:** follows along, opens the app to see the next flavor, has tried a drop or two. Emotionally bought into the craft.
- **Job to be done:** "I want to taste what's new and feel like I'm in on it."
- **Primary retention hook:** the drop cadence + the Cookie Passport — collection mechanics that reward trying everything.
- **Graduates when:** ordering frequently enough that Dough and tiers start to matter, or they don't want to miss a drop again.

### Frequent purchaser
- **Who:** orders regularly. Has real Dough balance, climbing tiers, rarely misses a drop.
- **Job to be done:** "KUKI is my treat — I want it to be easy, rewarding, and a little bit mine."
- **Primary retention hook:** Dough + tiered membership — accrued status and value that would feel wasteful to abandon.
- **Graduates when:** the math and the perks of guaranteed drop access make a subscription the obvious move.

### Subscription member (KUKI Club)
- **Who:** the top of the ladder. Monthly box, guaranteed drop allocation, early access. The superfan.
- **Job to be done:** "I want the full studio experience handled for me, and first access to everything."
- **Primary retention hook:** guaranteed allocation + members-only flavors + status — the things that make leaving feel like losing membership, not canceling a service.
- **Graduates to:** advocate. The referral and gifting loops turn this persona into a channel — they bring the next casual customers in.

## 3. Engagement loops

The loops that give the app a pulse between orders.

### The Drop
A limited, experimental flavor released on a **fixed cadence** (e.g. monthly), **app-first**, with a **countdown** and a finite run that **sells out**. The signature cookie is always available — that's the dependable core and never scarce. Drops are the variable layer that creates the scheduled return visit: people learn the rhythm and come back for it. Sell-outs are a feature, not a failure — scarcity is what makes the next one matter.

### Dough — the loyalty currency
**Dough** is earned on every order and redeemed for cookies and early access. *(Named to avoid "Crumbs," which Crumbl already owns; "Dough" doubles as the literal raw material and the slang for currency.)* Dough is the always-on reason to return — a balance that grows with you, funds the occasional free cookie, and feeds the tier system that unlocks status and access. It rewards the behavior we want (ordering, trying drops) without ever feeling like a points-grind.

### Cookie Passport
A **stamp for every flavor you've ever tried** — the signature plus each drop you caught. This does three things at once:

- Makes drops **compounding**: each one is a permanent addition to a collection, so missing one is missing a stamp forever.
- Creates a **shareable social artifact**: a beautiful passport is something enthusiasts post and compare — organic acquisition.
- Gives the app a **reason to open with zero purchase intent**: people check and admire collections.

### Bake tracking as delight
Live batch status, oven to door: *in the oven → on its way → warm at your door.* It reframes the wait as anticipation and makes a one-off transaction feel like an event. The single most repeatable moment of delight in the product, and the clearest signal that KUKI is a studio, not a kitchen ticket.

### What we deliberately avoid
**No dark-pattern streaks.** Daily-streak guilt and loss-aversion mechanics are off-brand for a premium product — they cheapen the relationship and punish the customer for living their life. Instead we use **"seasons"**: gentle, time-boxed framing (a season of drops, a seasonal passport page) that creates rhythm and freshness without manufacturing anxiety. We want people to *want* to come back, never to feel they'll be penalized if they don't.

## 4. Business opportunities

Revenue and growth surfaces that follow naturally from the membership model.

- **KUKI Club (subscription)** — the flagship. A monthly box plus **guaranteed drop allocation** and **early access**. Converts the brand's best customers into predictable recurring revenue and removes their single biggest pain (missing a drop).
- **Give-a-cookie / get-a-cookie referrals** — a gifting-shaped referral loop: send a friend a cookie, you both get one. Acquisition that rides on the product itself, powered by the advocate persona.
- **Tiered membership** — status earned through **Dough**. Higher tiers unlock perks (earlier access, bonus Dough, members-only flavors). Turns loyalty into a ladder people want to climb.
- **Members-only flavors** — drops reserved for Club members or top tiers. Deepens the value of membership and gives the top of the ladder something the rest can't get.
- **Gifting** — send a box or a cookie to someone else. Natural for a premium, giftable, single-SKU product; a clean acquisition channel and a strong seasonal revenue lever.

## 5. MVP recommendation

The **smallest version that creates retention**, not just the smallest version that takes an order. The bar for inclusion: does it give someone a reason to open the app *between* orders?

**In scope for v1:**

1. **Ordering** — the signature cookie, clean and fast. Table stakes.
2. **Live bake & delivery tracker** — oven → on its way → warm at door. The signature delight moment; cheap to make memorable.
3. **Dough** — earn on every order, redeem for cookies / early access. The always-on return reason.
4. **One app-exclusive monthly Drop** — a single recurring drop with a countdown and a finite run. Proves the scarcity loop with minimal operational load.
5. **Push notifications** — drop announcements and oven status. The mechanism that actually pulls people back; without it, the loops above are invisible.

**Explicitly deferred:** Cookie Passport, subscription, referrals, gifting, personalization, social. All valuable, none required to prove the core bet.

**Rationale:** ordering alone is a funnel and dies. Ordering + tracker + Dough + one Drop + push is the minimum combination that earns a place on the home screen — a reason to open the app on a Tuesday when you're not even hungry. That's the hypothesis the MVP exists to test.

## 6. Roadmap

### Phase 1 — Prove retention (the MVP)
Ordering, live bake/delivery tracker, Dough, one app-exclusive monthly Drop, push notifications. **Goal:** validate that scarcity + ritual + a balance produce between-order opens.

### Phase 2 — Deepen membership
Cookie Passport (collection mechanics + shareable artifact), KUKI Club subscription (monthly box, guaranteed allocation, early access), give-a-cookie referrals, gifting. **Goal:** convert engaged users into members and advocates; turn retention into recurring revenue and a growth loop.

### Phase 3 — Expand the world
Personalization (a taste profile that tailors drops and recommendations), seasonal **"collections"** (themed runs of drops with their own passport pages), a community/social layer (sharing, comparing, commentary), and an expansion surface (events, B2B/corporate gifting, new cities). **Goal:** broaden the studio beyond one customer ordering one cookie — without ever diluting the single-cookie soul.

## 7. Success metrics & North Star

**North Star: between-order engagement.** Specifically, **app opens (or sessions) per active user between purchases.** It captures the entire thesis in one number — if the app is genuinely membership and not a funnel, people open it when they aren't buying. GMV is an output we expect to follow; it is *not* the North Star, because optimizing for it directly pulls us back toward being a checkout funnel.

**Per-phase KPIs:**

- **Phase 1 (Prove retention):** first-order activation rate; **repeat-open rate** (the share of users who open the app without an immediate purchase); Drop **sell-through**; tracker engagement (share of orders where the tracker is opened); push opt-in and notification → open rate.
- **Phase 2 (Deepen membership):** Cookie Passport completion / stamps-per-user; **Club conversion and retention** (and churn); referral **K-factor**; gifting attach rate.
- **Phase 3 (Expand):** personalization lift (drop conversion with vs. without a taste profile); **season participation** rate; expansion-surface adoption (events / B2B / new-city take-up).

**Guardrails (so growth never costs the brand):** drop sell-out *time* (too fast = under-supplied and frustrating; never selling out = not scarce), notification unsubscribe rate (a proxy for whether we've crossed into nagging), and order-to-delivery warmth/quality. Premium is the moat — we watch the numbers that tell us if we're spending it.
